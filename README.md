# DSAN-5100-Final-Project
Group Members: Pavlos Baffes, Noelle Martell, Younghoon Kim, Nkemdibe Okweye, Nikhil Patla
Title:
Socioeconomic and Structural Determinants of Voter Turnout in U.S. Elections (2015–2025)
Topic Overview:
This project examines how socioeconomic and cultural factors influence voter turnout in the United States. The goal is to understand whether shifts in cost of living, income, education, crime, or other social conditions are associated with higher or lower electoral participation over time.
Focus:
The analysis will include country-level, state-level, and city-level elections across several key states. States under consideration include New York, Virginia, Pennsylvania, Georgia, Michigan, Nevada, North Carolina, Wisconsin, and Florida. The time frame (2015–2025)
Research Question:
What socioeconomic and cultural factors most strongly correlate with voter turnout across U.S. elections between 2015 and 2024?
